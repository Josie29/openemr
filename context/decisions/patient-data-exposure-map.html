01 Data by sensitivity tier

The same record set, ranked by the regulatory and breach risk it carries if it reaches an AI context window. Credentials should never enter one; the Critical clinical categories carry special legal protection (42 CFR Part 2, state super-confidentiality) beyond baseline HIPAA.

Critical

Identifiers, protected health status & credentials

Highest regulatory / breach exposure. Some must be excluded from AI ingestion entirely.

  • Government identifiersSSN (patient_data.ss), driver's license, DOB, full + birth/dead name, guardian block — all plaintext varchar.
  • Part 2 / special-protection healthHIV status (form_ros.hiv), substance use & mental-health history (history_data), therapy-group enrollment (therapy_groups*), controlled-substance Rx (erx_narcotics).
  • Reproductive / sexual health & SOGIContraception dates, gyn/breast/prostate exam history, sexual_orientation, gender_identity.
  • Auth credentialsPortal password hashes (patient_access_onsite.portal_pwd), staff users_secure.password*, MFA registrations. Never belongs in an AI prompt.
High

Financial & insurance PII

Identity-theft and payment exposure; subscriber data drags in a second person's identifiers.

  • Insurance / coverageinsurance_data: policy & group numbers, subscriber SSN + DOB, full subscriber name/address/employer, copay.
  • Billing & paymentsbilling, payments, patient balances, in_collection flags, billing notes.
  • Payment-processor secretspayment_gateway_details login/transaction keys. Note: no raw card / bank number is stored — card data lives with the external gateway.
Moderate

Unstructured free-text

Lowest baseline sensitivity but the highest ingestion risk — can silently contain any Critical item in narrative form.

  • Clinical narrativeSOAP/ROS notes, form_encounter.reason, lists.comments & diagnosis titles.
  • Messages & notespnotes.body (patient / portal messages), amendments, dictation.
  • History & documentshistory_data family/lifestyle longtext, documents.document_data + scanned files, generic lbf_data / usertext* buckets.

02 The data by clinical domain

Where the data actually lives — ~90–100 PHI-bearing tables grouped into the domains the service layer (src/Services/) models. Each domain has a matching FHIR projection under src/Services/FHIR/.

Identity & demographics

patient_data, person, patient_name_history, employer_data
Name, SSN, DOB, address, phone, email, race/ethnicity, religion, income, occupation, guardian.

Contacts & related persons

contact, contact_address, contact_telecom, phone_numbers, misc_address_book
Next-of-kin, guardians, addresses & telecoms for the patient and their relations.

Insurance & billing

insurance_data, insurance_numbers, billing, claims, payments, ar_activity
Policy/group #, subscriber SSN, coverage, charges, balances, payment ledger.

Encounters & notes

form_encounter, forms, form_soap, form_clinical_notes
Visit dates, provider, facility, reason, and free-text SOAP / clinical narrative.

Problems & conditions

lists, issue_encounter, issue_types
Diagnoses, allergies, medications & sensitive issues (HIV, behavioral health) as list records.

Medications & allergies

prescriptions, drugs, drug_sales, erx_narcotics
Drug, dose, refills, prescriber, indication — incl. controlled substances & dispensing.

Labs, procedures & vitals

procedure_order, procedure_result, form_vitals, form_observation
Lab results, orders, specimens, surgical history, BP/weight/BMI and observations.

Immunizations & devices

immunizations, immunization_observation, device
Vaccination history, refusal reasons, implantable-device UDIs.

Social history & SDOH

history_data, form_history_sdoh, therapy_groups*
Tobacco/alcohol/drug use, mental-illness family history, SOGI, housing/food risk, therapy enrollment.

Documents & media

documents, categories_to_documents, onsite_documents
Scanned records, IDs, photos, CCDAs — arbitrary uploaded PHI, encryption optional per-row.

Appointments & tracking

openemr_postcalendar_events, patient_tracker, dated_reminders
Appointment reason/status/time, in-clinic flow, chart location, reminders.

Messaging & portal

pnotes, onsite_messages, patient_access_onsite
Secure messages (encryptable) and patient portal login + password hash.

Questionnaires & screening

questionnaire_response, pro_assessments, form_functional_cognitive_status
Screening answers (incl. behavioral-health / PHQ), functional & cognitive assessments.

Audit & access logs

log, audit_master, extended_log, api_log
Every PHI read/write — comment/query payloads can embed PHI & IP addresses.

04 The built-in guardrail an AI must honor

OpenEMR already ships one data-sensitivity access control. Any AI retrieval layer that queries tables directly — rather than going through the service layer — will silently bypass it.

Encounter-level sensitivity ACL

form_encounter.sensitivity tags an encounter normal or high. The sensitivities ACO section (created at install) walls high encounters off from users who lack that grant. EncounterService enforces it via AclMain::aclCheckCore('sensitivities', …) — an AI pipeline reading form_encounter directly leaks encounters the current user is legally walled off from.

  • Consent flags on patient_data (hipaa_*, allow_health_info_ex, allow_patient_portal) express patient disclosure constraints the co-pilot should treat as hard limits.
  • Per-row encryption flags exist (documents.encrypted, pnotes.is_msg_encrypted) but default off — encryption is not a reliable filter for "is this sensitive".
  • Prior audit's IDOR finding compounds this: authorization is role-scoped, not patient-scoped — any authenticated user with a capability can already fetch any patient by pid.

05 Cross-check against the prior AUDIT.md

This map was re-derived independently, then compared. The prior audit is a five-dimension system audit (security, compliance, performance, data quality, architecture) — it touches PHI but never enumerates the full data inventory. The two are complementary, and they agree where they overlap.

✓ Confirmed — both agree

  • Core PHI tables (patient_data, lists, insurance_data, documents) are the sensitive spine.
  • SSN stored plaintext; column encryption is opt-in / off by default.
  • FHIR + REST + Bulk $export are real outbound PHI surfaces & BAA triggers.
  • Cross-patient reach: audit's IDOR = our getAll / $export whole-population paths.

+ New in this map

  • Full inventory: ~90–100 PHI tables of 282 (audit named only the hot ones).
  • The form_encounter.sensitivity ACO guardrail — not called out in the audit.
  • Explicit Part 2 / super-confidential map: HIV, therapy_groups, erx_narcotics, SOGI, reproductive health.
  • Free-text ingestion risk surface (pnotes.body, history_data, document_data) enumerated for AI scrubbing.

⚠ In the audit, not a data-map concern

  • Live-DB reality: 25 synthetic patients, contact fields 0% populated, medication coding mismatch.
  • No patient de-duplication — ingestion can silently fork a person into duplicate records.
  • Audit-on-read logging: every PHI SELECT writes 2 rows — an AI read-heavy pipeline amplifies this.
  • Committed secrets, XSS/autoescape, session-cookie posture — security findings orthogonal to the inventory.
